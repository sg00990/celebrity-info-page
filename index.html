Kim Namjoon

1994 - Present

Leader of the Biggest Band in the 21st Century

Kim Nam-joon (Korean: 김남준; born September 12, 1994), [1] better known by his stage name RM (formerly Rap Monster), is a South Korean rapper, songwriter and record producer. He is the leader of the South Korean boy group BTS. In 2015, he released his first solo mixtape, RM. In October 2018, his second mixtape, Mono, was the highest-charting album by a Korean soloist on the Billboard 200 chart, having peaked at number 26. He has recorded with artists such as Wale, Younha, Warren G, Gaeko, Krizz Kaliko, MFBTY, Fall Out Boy, Primary, and Lil Nas X.

The Following is a Timeline of Namjoon's Life

  • 1994 - born in Dongjak-gu, South Korea.
  • 2010 - became the first trainee at Big Hit.
  • 2013 - debuted with six others as BTS.
  • 2015 - released his first mixtape, 'RM'.
  • 2015 - BTS won their first award.
  • 2016 - BTS won Album of the Year for the first time.
  • 2017 - performed at their first American award show, BBMAs.
  • 2018 - released his second mixtape, 'Mono'.
  • 2018 - spoke at the United Nations General Assembly for the first time.
  • 2018 - awarded the fifth-class Hwagwan Order of Cultural Merit by the President of South Korea.
  • 2020 - nominated for their first Grammy.
  • 2021 - performed and spoke at the United Nations General Assembly.
